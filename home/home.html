history of origami


Origami (Japanese: 折り紙, Japanese pronunciation: [oɾiɡami] or [oɾiꜜɡami]) is an art of paper folding that originated in Japan. The Japanese word origami is derived from two words: ori means folded or folded and kami means paper. Origami has only been used since 1880; Before that, the Japanese used orikata. Origami combines simple folding ways to turn rectangular (2-dimensional) pieces of paper, which are usually squares, into complex shapes (3-dimensional), without collage in the folding process, this is also the trend of Modern origami. Contrary to popular belief, traditional Japanese origami rules (starting around the Edo period 1603-1867)[1], are less strict than modern origami: folded paper can be circles, triangles, or circles. sense, and can be collaged during folding.
