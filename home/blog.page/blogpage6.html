Remembering those lost to Covid-19

On July 31, 2020, the first Covid-19 death in Vietnam was a 70-year-old patient from Quang Nam who was being treated for chronic kidney failure at Da Nang hospital. The second outbreak in Da Nang recorded an additional 34 deaths in 501 positive cases.
In January 2021, the four-third wave of the epidemic broke out in Quang Ninh, Hai Duong and then spread but was soon controlled. No people were killed. Vietnam is considered successful in fighting the epidemic by the world.

Content

In order to commemorate the losses caused by the Covid-19 pandemic in Vietnam, The Central Committee of the Vietnam Fatherland Front in collaboration with the Ho Chi Minh City City Committee organized a memorial ceremony for the dead compatriots and soldiers who died during the Covid-19 pandemic at The Reunification Hall, TPHCM at 20:00 tonight. At the same time, at 20:30, religious institutions rang the memorial bell.
Memorial to compatriots who died and officers and soldiers who died due to the Covid-19 pandemic: join hands to turn off the lights, light candles in a moment of remembrance.

At the same time, the unnamed artist also commemorated his deceased father by creating a symbolic paper skull and prayed that he would rest in the golden spring.
