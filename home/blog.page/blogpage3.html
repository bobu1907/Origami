Koi fish - Symbol in Japanese culture

Koi fish, also known in pure Vietnamese, is Japanese carp, and in Japanese is Nishikigoi. Originally Koi fish originated in China, but 200 years ago the Japanese bred the breed into a completely different fish, and became an icon in Japanese culture.

Japanese mascot

Untamed carp will usually be brown or blue, but when bred it is extremely vibrant and rich in color. The most common are white, red, yellow, silver gray and depending on the place there are different names such as Kohaku, Yamabuki, Kuchibeni, Ogon, San Showa, Kin Showa, Tancho,... And each name has different meanings: Kohaku has a combination of white and red spots that mean career success. Yamabuki's yellow color means wealth.

Meaning

In Japanese culture, Koi fish is a symbol of a strong and energetic vitality, like the ability to swim against the current to lay eggs. It is a symbol of success and courage, patience to overcome difficult challenges in life.
